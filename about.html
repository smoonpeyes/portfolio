In Brief

With 20+ years of experience in service design, product design, accessibility, and user research, I help organizations create thoughtful, inclusive products that improve lives at scale.

I've worked across industries -- from retail and healthcare to enterprise and transportation -- leading teams, shaping process, and embedding accessibility into every stage of product development. I believe good design is not only about solving problems, but also about building trust, clarity, and connection.

My Differentiators

I'm a builder of foundations...

I don't just design products—I build the infrastructure that makes good design possible at scale. At HEB, I started the digital accessibility program from scratch. I know how to identify gaps, make the case for investment, and create systems that outlast any single project. I care about leaving things better than I found them.

I'm a translator...

I spent 20+ years moving between highly technical stakeholders (pilots, flight attendants, engineers) and everyday users (grocery shoppers, families). I can take complex regulatory requirements or business constraints and turn them into intuitive experiences. No jargon, no confusion—just clarity.

I'm a drummer...

When you're the drummer in a band, you're not the star—you're the backbone. You set the tempo, you keep everyone together, and if you mess up, the whole song falls apart. I bring that same energy to design: I know when to lead, when to support, and how to keep a team in sync even when things get chaotic.

I'm a runner...

Trail running teaches you that the path is never as straightforward as you think. There are switchbacks, obstacles, and moments where you question why you're doing this at all. But you keep moving forward, you adapt to the terrain, and you celebrate when you reach the summit. That's how I approach complex design challenges—with endurance, adaptability, and an eye on the long game.

I'm a storyteller...

Before I was a designer, I wanted to be a screenwriter (still do, honestly). That training taught me that everything is about the narrative: Why does this button matter? What's the user's emotional journey? How do we make stakeholders care? I bring that storytelling lens to everything—from design critiques to executive presentations.

I'm an advocate...

I've been the one in the room saying "but what about accessibility?" or "have we thought about edge cases?" for my entire career. Good design doesn't just work for the happy path—it works for everyone, including the people who get forgotten. I don't just check boxes; I genuinely care about inclusive design because everyone deserves to be picked for the team of life.
